Overview

As part of the Mercedes-Benz China HR digitalization initiative, I designed and led a comprehensive digital transformation bootcamp spanning two rounds across 15 months. The program targeted HR professionals across Greater China, equipping them with hands-on Power BI, Power Automate, and Microsoft 365 skills to transition from manual, spreadsheet-based workflows to self-service analytics and automated reporting.

The program was recognized by Mercedes-Benz China HR executive leadership and the results were presented directly to Mercedes-Benz Board Member Sabine Kohleisen, marking it as one of the most impactful digital upskilling initiatives within the China HR division.

Program Design & Curriculum

The bootcamp curriculum was structured as a progressive learning journey — moving from foundational data literacy to advanced dashboard development. Each module combined live instruction, hands-on labs, and real-world HR data exercises to ensure participants could immediately apply skills to their daily workflows.

Dashboard Development

Participants built functional Power BI dashboards using real Mercedes-Benz HR data throughout the program. These dashboards covered headcount analytics, attrition forecasting, recruitment pipeline tracking, and compensation benchmarking — directly replacing legacy Excel-based reporting processes.

Participant Engagement & Feedback

Across both rounds, the program maintained high engagement with strong completion rates and positive feedback from participants. Automation & Process Improvement

Beyond dashboards, the program introduced Power Automate flows to streamline repetitive HR tasks — automated approval chains, scheduled report distribution, and data refresh pipelines. These automations collectively reduced manual reporting effort and freed up time for strategic analysis.

The digital transformation bootcamp produced measurable, lasting change within the Mercedes-Benz China HR organization. The shift from manual Excel workflows to self-service Power BI analytics fundamentally changed how the team approaches data-driven decision-making.
